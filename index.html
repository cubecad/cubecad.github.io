HiP-CAD

Through the Hour Glass: Hierarchical Token Pooling Transformers for CAD Reverse Engineering

Altay Kacan1, Youssef Youssef1,2, Bertram Fuchs1, Oliver Lohse1, Stefan Goetz3, Sandro Wartzack3,
1Siemens AG,
2Technical University of Munich,
3Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg

HiP-CAD efficiently generates 3D CAD models with full history from point clouds.

Abstract

3D Computer-Aided Design (CAD) reverse engineering aims to recover parametric design intent from raw geometric data, such as point clouds or Boundary Representations (B-Reps). While recent Large Language Model (LLM) based approaches have demonstrated high reconstruction accuracy, most previous transformer-based methods typically treat CAD programs as flat token sequences within the transformer blocks, ignoring the inherent hierarchical structure of commands, loops, and features. This representation mismatch leads to computational inefficiencies, as standard transformer architectures scale quadratically with sequence length

We propose HiP-CAD(Hierarchical- Pooling Transformer for CAD), a novel hourglass architecture that embeds the structural hierarchy of CAD programs directly into the model’s architecture. By utilizing token pooling based on CAD program grammar and causal upsampling, HiP-CAD progressively reduces sequence resolution in intermediate layers, enabling the model to reason over abstract semantic units rather than individual tokens.

Our results demonstrate that HiP-CAD achieves state-of-the-art reconstruction accuracy among non-LLM methods on the DeepCAD test set, rivaling the performance of 2-billion-parameter LLMs while utilizing only 440M parameters. Furthermore, the hierarchical pooling mechanism reduces peak GPU memory usage by a factor of three and significantly accelerates training, allowing for end-to-end optimization on a single workstation GPU. We further validate our approach through the first cross-dataset evaluation of a non-LLM architecture on the SynthBal-1M and CAD-Recode datasets, highlighting the critical role of hierarchy-aware inductive biases in scalable CAD generation.
