Abstract

Neutral exchange formats for 3D CAD such as STEP enable geometric export but do not preserve the model histories required for modification, forcing CAD experts to manually rebuild editable models. This paper presents CubeCAD, a novel AI-driven framework for reverse engineering parametric 3D CAD models from boundary representation (B-Rep) geometries. The proposed coarse-to-fine approach first decomposes input 3D geometry into cuboid primitives, then recognizes symmetries and predicts construction paths and sketch profiles for fine-grained reconstruction. The framework generates variable-length 3D CAD construction sequences that dynamically adapt to shape complexity while explicitly discovering and preserving reflective and rotational symmetries.

Operating without manual annotations or labeled training data, CubeCAD is fully unsupervised and scalable for industrial applications, though it can be extended to leverage human-annotated construction sequences in supervised settings when available. The vendor-independent neural representations of 3D CAD model history can be adapted to commercial CAD platforms, enabling fully editable parametric models with complete construction histories.

Validation on ShapeNet demonstrates substantial improvements over the state-of-the-art: 9.7% lower mean Chamfer Distance for chairs, 39.2% reduction in unique primitives through effective symmetry learning, and dramatic multi-category gains (58.4% for chairs, 55.8% for tables, 33.8% for sofas) using 30% fewer primitives on average. Additionally, experiments on the ABC mechanical parts dataset confirm state-of-the-art performance with clear advantages in multi-category training. The method maintains robust generalization across categories, and qualitative analysis confirms semantically meaningful construction sequences aligned with human 3D CAD modeling workflows.
